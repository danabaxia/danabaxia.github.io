About

I'm a manufacturing test engineer at Enphase Energy, working on the IQ Battery R4 production line. My day-to-day involves building and maintaining the test systems that validate every battery unit before it ships.

This blog documents the tools, patterns, and lessons from that work — particularly at the intersection of AI-assisted development, embedded systems testing, and manufacturing automation.

What I Work On

  • Manufacturing test frameworks — Python-based systems that orchestrate test sequences, communicate with hardware over CAN bus, and determine pass/fail on the production line
  • AI-assisted validation — Using Claude Code as a development partner to build, test, and validate changes without requiring physical hardware
  • Virtual test runners — Simulation layers that replicate hardware behavior from golden unit profiles, enabling full test validation on a laptop
  • State machine design — Formal models for battery charge/discharge states, transition validation, and per-device tracking

Why This Blog

Manufacturing test engineering doesn't get written about much. The work sits at a unique intersection: you need hardware domain knowledge, software engineering skills, and an understanding of production constraints (throughput, reliability, cost). When AI agents enter this picture, interesting things happen.

I write here to share what works, what doesn't, and what I wish someone had told me.
